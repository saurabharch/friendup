Voice Command in FriendUP (under review)

Introduction

In FriendUP, you can use the microphone on your computer, phone, TV or tablet to control the whole system. This is possible, because the whole of Friend is based on language. Everything you can do in a Command Line Interface (CLI), you can do using proper spoken language through your sound input device. This opens up a whole new world of usability.

In this document, we will go through the most common scenarios where the voice command function is useful. We will start on a simple level, then advance you to the more complex scenarios later on.

In the most basic use, Friend takes commands, consisting of one, two or three words in combination. In the more advanced use, you also utilize contexts and sessions. We will get back to these later, but rest assured, with Friend, you have got the whole system at the mercy of your lips.

Technical setup

Before we get started, there are some important technical factors to consider. First; the only web browsers that support voice recognition at this time is Google Chrome and Samsung Internet. While this covers all platforms, it might not apply to your own default browser. While FriendUP supports Safari in most use cases, Apple does not approve of third party web applications having access to any microphone input.

In other words, for FriendUP to work with voice, please use Samsung Internet or Google Chrome for now.

Another factor is your microphone and speaker setup. In FriendUP, we use voice synthesis to guide you with voice. But as we are continuously listening on your microphone to get your voice input, your speakers might be picked up instead of your voice. To prevent this, please either wear a headset, or turn the volume on your speakers down. Additionally, make sure your microphone input is moderated, so that the volume is not too high, not too low.

Once you have found a setup where the speakers have a low volume, or are unheard by your microphone, you should be ready to get started.

Getting started

Controlling FriendUP through voice is like a game of words. To make your voice commands intelligible to Friend, we have created some rules you need to follow. We have been flexible in how we interpret your voice, so you always have two or three ways of accomplishing the same thing. This makes it a bit more comforable for you, if the voice recognition technology has a problem with understanding your pronounciation.

When starting out, look for the microphone icon on the bottom right part of your screen. Click on it to activate the voice recognition. You will be alerted that the voice recognition is active.

The first thing you might do, is to access the volumes on your desktop (or the volumes hidden away in your volume dock, on mobile).

Open the system volume like this:

open volume system

Friend also can reach the volume by count. Considering you have two volume icons, Home: and then System:, the System volume would be volume two. In this case, you can say:

open volume two or open second volume

When you have done this, assuming the voice recognition works, you the System: directory will open in a window in front of you. You can now access the files and directories listed. To open the Functions directory, say:

open third directory or open directory three or open directory functions

This will open the Functions directory in a new window, activated in front of you.

Now we have a list of functions in front of you. We use some basic parsing to match with the filenames (because the voice recognition might not know exactly what you are saying, letter by letter). Say:

open file two or open second file

Most probably, the file name is unintelligible to the voice recognition software. But you could try:

open file screen open (which we try to combine to "screenopen")

By now, a new unnamed screen should open. And you might want to switch back to the Workspace screen. Do this by saying:

screen switch or screen swap

You can also switch back to the opened screen by saying:

screen switch or screen swap (once again)

Now you can close the screen by saying:

screen close

Now you know how to open volumes, directories and files. This will get you very far, as you now also know how to execute Dormant functions in for example Artisan, the programming application. Let us open this application. (Make sure you ran it once before, because you need to install and/or activate the application prior to using it.)

With FriendUP, we understood that application names might be difficult to understand for the voice recognition software. You might have names like Shoutcast, or k3b, which will be next to impossible to match with any dictionary. Because of this, we have pre programmed some aliases that the voice recognition software will understand. So to open up Artisan, the programming application, you say:

open programming application or run programming application

This runs the application, and the name of the application will be spoken by the speech synthesis software. After this, an application volume icon will appear on your Workspace. Open this by the correct sort order number.

open volume one or open first volume

This opens up the Artisan Dormant volume window. Here you can access the Artisan functions. Then say:

open directory functions

Now, in the Functions directory, you will get a lot of dormant functions listed. You can scroll down to see all of them.

scroll down

scroll down

scroll down

You should now see the Insert function. This one we want to use to put some text into Artisan. Say:

open file insert

The speech synthesis will now ask you for arguments, as the Insert function is executable. Say:

just saying that the weather could be better

Now, the text should be put into Artisan. The synthetic voice will ask if you want to do more, or just stop. Say:

stop

Now the synthetic voice will tell you that the speech recognition has gone back into standard mode. Say:

switch windows

You can say this until Artisan is the window in the foreground. When you are happy with the results, say:

close windows

This will close all open windows and leave you with a clean Workspace. Please look at the Voice Regocnition specification for all the voice commands.
